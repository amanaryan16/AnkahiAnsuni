Peace

Learn to accept that there are other people around, other kind of thoughts, other beliefs, other religions and that is okay. our most valuable ownership is an open mind. Peace means living a life where your rights are guaranteed. A life in which your successes and failures are the result of your own actions, not neglect of the will of others. Peace is forgiveness. It only exists when you smile at the person who has made you go through a difficult situation. Peace is saying “let us move on”. Monika, Slovakia

Peace is our goal in life, we keep trying to find it, to make rules for it, to make wars to achieve it. but what really peace is? Peace is just to be humans living on the mother earth with no other differences trying to do the best for the whole world not only for ourselves till we reach the inevitable moment when we say goodbye to this world with satisfied Conscience or a peaceful one. Hussein, Iraq

I look at the world and don’t see justice. I consider the reality lived in many countries and the word democracy sounds like a poorly told fable. Peace means to me that people won’t starve and will have a secure place to sleep to call their own. Peace means equal opportunities to develop as a social and intellectual being; equal possibilities of growing up to enjoy adulthood and old age with dignity. Peace is work and freedom of movement. Peace is the recognition of love between two people as legit regardless of their orientation, and also exercising the rights to your own body. Peace is true freedom of creed and education without indoctrination. Peace is nonviolence, just wages, working class vindications and access to potable water and quality health and sanitary services. Peace is preventive medicine. Gender equality in every context and being able to walk through your neighborhood without fear of being attacked on the street is peace. Peace is decolonization and zero neocolonialism. Peace is fair trade and cooperativism. Peace is more books, no guns and less marketing and political propaganda. Peace is social and cultural contributions, development and art. Peace is leisure time, belonging, equality, respect, dignity and justice. Freedom of speech. Peace is loving humanity as an action verb and the constant quests of being: existence, society and the environment. Peace is having healthy relationships. Peace is within and towards everyone. Ana Pamela, Dominican Republic
